# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make Heptapus Open Code Organization a respectful, serious, and welcoming place for people who build and study open-code systems.

We welcome participation regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or background.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps the community:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting different experience levels and learning speeds.
- Giving technical feedback that is specific, actionable, and focused on the work.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and correcting mistakes.
- Documenting decisions and trade-offs so others can follow the reasoning.
- Crediting contributions fairly.
- Being honest about limitations, risks, and project maturity.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, slurs, or demeaning comments.
- Sexualized language or imagery where it is not clearly relevant to the project.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Plagiarism, license misuse, or intentional removal of attribution.
- Misrepresenting experimental work as production-safe.
- Pressuring maintainers or contributors for unpaid labor outside reasonable project norms.
- Introducing intentionally malicious code, backdoors, credential theft, or unsafe hidden behavior.

## Engineering Ethics

Because this organization works on systems software and other infrastructure-like projects, we hold ourselves to these additional standards:

- Security problems should be reported responsibly and fixed transparently.
- Dangerous behavior must be documented clearly, especially in kernels, drivers, networking, cryptography, automation, AI, and quantum-control code.
- Benchmarks, screenshots, demos, and claims should not be fabricated.
- Licenses and third-party dependencies must be respected.
- Contributors should avoid adding opaque complexity where a clear design would serve the project better.
- Maintainers should distinguish experiments, prototypes, and stable releases.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for behavior they determine to be harmful, unsafe, or inappropriate.

## Reporting

Report concerns privately to the maintainers listed in the relevant repository. If no repository-specific contact exists, open a private communication channel with the organization owner or maintainers.

Reports will be handled with care. We will consider context, impact, available evidence, and the safety of the people involved.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in organization repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, project spaces, chats, events, and any public context where someone is representing the organization or its projects.
